How to go live
▶️
YouTube Live
youtube.com/live/VIDEO_ID
  1. Open YouTube Studio and click Go Live in the top-right corner.
  2. Choose Stream (for OBS) or Webcam for a quick browser-based broadcast.
  3. Set your title and click Go Live. YouTube will give you a live URL like youtube.com/live/AbCdEfG1234.
  4. Copy that URL and paste it into the field above.
OBS users: In YouTube Studio, copy the Stream key and paste it into OBS under Settings → Stream → YouTube RTMPS.
🟣
Twitch
twitch.tv/your_channel
  1. Open OBS Studio (or Streamlabs) on your laptop or phone.
  2. Go to Settings → Stream, choose Twitch, and connect your account.
  3. Click Start Streaming in OBS.
  4. Your stream URL is simply twitch.tv/your_channel_name — paste that above.
No account yet? Sign up free at twitch.tv. Your channel URL is available immediately.
📹
OBS / Custom HLS
https://your-server.com/stream.m3u8
  1. In OBS Studio, go to Settings → Output → Recording and set the format to HLS.
  2. Or use a self-hosted media server (e.g. nginx-rtmp, SRS, or MediaMTX) and point OBS at it via RTMP.
  3. The server will produce an .m3u8 playlist URL — something like https://yourserver.com/live/game.m3u8.
  4. Paste that .m3u8 URL into the stream URL field above.
Quick local option: Run mediamtx on your laptop and push with OBS via RTMP. It outputs an HLS URL accessible on your local network.
